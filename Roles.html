Digital Construction Roles

Digital Construction Manager

The Digital Construction Manager oversees digital processes in construction projects, focusing on efficient delivery through tools such as three-dimensional digital twins and common data environments (CDEs). The role enhances collaboration, reduces risks, and optimises outcomes in cost, time, and quality.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Model Management: Use 3D models for site briefings and visual presentations, ensuring teams understand tasks and design intent.
  • Digital Integration: Digital forms and snagging processes linked to the model simplify on-site tasks and improve issue tracking.
  • Project Information Alignment: Shared, clash-free models align all disciplines, reducing errors and costly rework.
  • Risk Mitigation: Tools like augmented reality provide real-time comparisons of design versus construction, enhancing quality control.
  • Decision Support: Providing accurate data for informed decision-making.
  • Collaboration: Facilitating communication across multidisciplinary teams.
  • Process Innovation: Promoting efficient, innovative digital approaches.
  • Training: Supporting teams in using digital tools and workflows effectively.

Digital Design Team Coordinator

The Design Team Coordinator focuses on managing and coordinating digital processes for specific tasks within the design phase of construction projects. This role ensures the accurate development of digital models and supports seamless collaboration within their assigned area to contribute to overall project success.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Task-Specific Model Coordination: Managing the development and accuracy of digital models for specific tasks.
  • Focused Collaboration: Coordinating with other team members and disciplines to ensure task alignment.
  • Standards Compliance: Ensuring workflows meet project information requirements for the assigned task.
  • Data Handling: Maintaining and organising task-related data in common data environments (CDEs).
  • Clash Detection: Identifying and resolving potential design conflicts within the assigned scope.
  • Tool Utilisation: Supporting the effective use of digital tools for task-specific design accuracy.
  • Guidance: Providing task-level support and guidance on digital workflows to team members.

Digital Site Coordinator

The Digital Site Coordinator manages the use of digital tools and models during site activities, ensuring they support construction processes and accurately incorporate site and as-built information. This role ensures models remain updated and aligned with real-world conditions.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Model Integration: Managing digital models to support site activities and construction workflows.
  • As-Built Updates: Ensuring site and as-built information is accurately added to the model.
  • Collaboration: Bridging site and design teams to maintain alignment and communication.
  • Data Accuracy: Verifying models reflect site conditions and changes during construction.
  • Clash Management: Identifying and resolving issues arising on-site.
  • Tool Optimisation: Supporting the effective use of digital tools on-site.
  • Documentation: Ensuring as-built documentation aligns with the final model.
